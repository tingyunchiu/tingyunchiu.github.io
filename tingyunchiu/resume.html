Research and Teaching Assistant at NYU

Studying political psychology at NYU is the turning point in my life!
My master's thesis was developed to answer the question: How do one's attitudes toward tradition intertwined with multi-political identities to impact supports for political movements? More concretely, will attitudes toward traditional gender roles impact support for LGBT rights, especially among sexual minorities?
I analyzed more than 400 questionnaire responses with R. Here's my simple answer: gay men with a more traditional gender role attitude still showed strong support for LGBT rights, but lesbian women did not. Why is this research important? This research tells you that the dynamic within one's political identities and political behaviors are more complicated than previous research has shown. More importantly, using a single political identity to attract support for societal changes might not be as effective as expected.

Being a teaching assistant to a group of 30 undergraduate intro-psychology students is challenging but fun. Every week, I led discussions on various topics in psychology and provided advice to my students. Receiving thank-you notes or emails from my students at the end of the semester is definitely one of the most satisfying moments.
